About Us

Project Jump was started by two college students that needed students in other majors for thier teams. Project Jump became a connection site that solves the difficulty of finding other students interested in your project. Whether you're looking for a project to work on with others, or new members for your team, Project Jump is the place to be.

Knight Hacks III

Knight Hacks Hackathon 2019, Lead Organizer

What is a hackathon? A hackathon is a 36-hour event in which students from all over the nation come together to create software/hardware projects. I am currently the lead organizer of the Knight Hacks III hackathon. This event is scheduled for March 1-3, 2019.
Knight Hacks started back in 2016, with 100+ student attendees. For Knight Hacks II, we had 300+ students in attendance and 50+ projects submitted! I took the role of Volunteers/Mentors Coordinator for that hackathon. For next year, I took the opportunity to be the leader of Knight Hacks III. We are expecting to have 500+ students. Our goal for this hackathon is to be able to connect students with career opportunities.

Expiry Web App

Expiry Web Application, Walmart API Use

As part of a Hackathon at the University of Florida, SwampHacks, my team and I have created a web app for people to be aware of expiration dates from food products to not waste food. The website uses the Walmart API to search in their extensive catalog of products with the product’s UPC code. It gives back information of the product, image, and the expected amount of days in which your food product can go bad. For the demo, we used XAMPP and ran it locally.
